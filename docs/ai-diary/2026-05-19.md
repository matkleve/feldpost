## What motivated the good work

Not generic “try harder” — a few concrete things stacked:

1. **A reference, not a mood board.** You pointed at the address row and named the mechanics: content width and height must not jump. That turns polish into an invariant I can test against instead of guessing what “nice” means.

2. **Pushback that tightened the system.** When I reached for a one-off helper, you steered back to `SearchBarService`. That forced the fix to live where the next bug will look — not in a graveyard of helpers.

3. **A long thread with memory.** Corrections (dropdown anchoring, `stephansdom`, EXIF vs resolved coords, delete on uploaded) accumulated into a picture of the panel’s logic. Later changes weren’t isolated tickets; they were finishing one surface.

4. **The repo’s contracts.** AGENTS, i18n pipeline, detail-row slot grid — constraints that say “don’t ship a local hack.” Following them is slower per edit and faster per week.

5. **Scope with a finish line.** “Copy address behavior to Captured, Projects, location fields, metadata dual inputs” is big but bounded. Bounded work is easier to do carefully than endless UI tweaks.

6. **Honest feedback at the end.** “Awesome” after bed plans isn’t a performance review — but it confirms the invariant matched your eye. That closes the loop and makes the next session worth the same care.

Underneath: treating Feldpost as a product someone uses in the field, not a component gallery. Stable inputs in a detail panel are boring until they’re wrong; you cared about the boring part.
